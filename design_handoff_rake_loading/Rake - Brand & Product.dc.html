The idea

Rake is premium, trustworthy, and data-first — a scoreboard you can bet on. The identity earns that with restraint: warm paper, deep ink, one confident blue, and figures that always line up.

The mark

A dot-matrix R — the scoreboard, distilled

The R is drawn on a 5×7 dot grid — a nod to stadium boards and box scores, kept flat and calm (no glow, no neon). It carries the capital so the wordmark can stay lowercase.

{{ gridBlue }}
Construction. Five columns, seven rows, round dots on a fixed grid. Lit dots form the letter; the grid never changes — only the color and the dot size do.

Sticky brand bar

Wordmark + LIVE stay pinned as you scroll; the league name scrolls away. You always know where you are and that the numbers are live.

Anchor nav, not tabs

Awards / Standings / Splits pins under the header and jumps down the same page. A long scroll stays navigable without becoming three separate screens.

Section rhythm

Thin sand bands break the scroll into chapters so it reads as three sections, not one endless list.

Standings go two-line

Team + record on top, a stat strip below — nothing clips off the right edge, and the leader in each category stays blue.

Close the scroll

A splits preview ends the page on something alive instead of a table that just stops.

Loading

While the numbers land

Small and quiet. A single dot-icon flips through the set — field, game, gaining, title — while one line, picked at random per load, shimmers underneath. On brand, and it buys a beat without feeling like dead time.
